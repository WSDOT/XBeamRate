1 Computing Column Height and Elevations {#computing_column_height_and_elevations}
2 ======================================
3 The height of columns and elevations are established from the input parameters. The sketch below shows the basic dimensions involved in the calculations.
4 ![](columnelevation.png)
5 
6 ##Top of Column Elevation
7 The top of column elevation is computed as
8 
9 Ytop = (Deck Elevation at Alignment) + (Xcol)(S) - TS - Hsuper - Hsub
10 
11 where
12 
13 Ytop = top of column elevation
14 
15 Xcol = offset from the alignment centerline to the subject column
16 
17 S = slope of the roadway surface in the plane of the pier
18 
19 TS = thickness of the deck slab measured to the top of the superstructure diaphragm
20 
21 Hsuper = depth of the superstructure (excluding the deck slab)
22 
23 Hsub = height of the cross beam (substructure depth)
24 
25 
26 ##Bottom of Column Elevation
27 When the column height is input, the bottom of column elevation is computed as
28 
29 Ybot = Ytop - Hcolumn
30 
31 where
32 
33 Ytop = top of column elevation
34 
35 Hcolumn = input height of column
36 
37 ##Column Height
38 When the bottom of column elevation is input, the column height is computed as
39 
40 Hcolumn = Ytop - Ybot.
41 
42 > NOTE: When integrated with PGSuper/PGSplice, the depth of the superstructure (Hsuper) is taken to be the depth of the tallest precast girder at the pier. The dimensions of oak blocks, bearing pads, grout pads, girder recesses, etc. are not taking into account. For this reason, you are advised to use more precise methods of determining the bottom of column elevation for purposes other than the pier model used by XBRate. **DO NOT USE THE COMPUTED BOTTOM OF COLUMN ELEVATION FOR PS&E OR CONSTRUCTION**
