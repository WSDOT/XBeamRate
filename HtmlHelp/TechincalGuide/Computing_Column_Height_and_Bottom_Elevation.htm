Computing Column Height and Elevations

The height of columns and elevations are established from the input parameters. The sketch below shows the basic dimensions involved in the calculations.

Top of Column Elevation

The top of column elevation is computed as (Deck elevation at Alignment) + (Xcol)(S) - TS - Hsuper - Hsub.

Xcol is the offset from the alignment to the centerline of the subject column

S is the slope of the roadway surface in the plane of the pier

TS is the thickness of the deck slab measured to the top of the superstructure diaphragm

Hsuper is the depth of the superstructure (excluding the deck slab)

Hsub is the depth of the cross beam (substructure depth)

Bottom of Column Elevation

When the column height is input, the bottom of column elevation is computed as (Top of Column Elevation) - Hcolumn.

Hcolumn is the height of column that was input.

Column Height

When the bottom of column elevation is input, the column height is computed as Hcolumn = (Top of Column Elevation) - (Bottom of Column Elevation)

NOTE: When integrated with PGSuper/PGSplice, the depth of the superstructure (Hsuper) is taken to be the depth of the tallest precast girder at the pier. The dimensions of oak blocks, bearing pads, grout pads, girder recesses, etc. are not taken into account. For this reason, you are advised to use more precise method of determining the bottom of column elevation for purposes other than the pier model used by XBRate. DO NOT USE THE BOTTOM OF COLUMN ELEVATION FOR PS&E OR CONSTRUCTION.
